Leopard Habitats

Horton Plains National Park

Located in the central highlands of Sri Lanka, Horton Plains National Park is known for its diverse ecosystems, including montane grasslands and cloud forests. The park is home to a population of Sri Lankan leopards. The unique landscape, characterized by cliffs, waterfalls, and scenic plains, provides an ideal habitat for leopards. Visitors to Horton Plains may have the opportunity to spot leopards roaming the grasslands or resting in the forested areas.

Knuckles Mountain Range

The Knuckles Mountain Range, also known as the Knuckles Massif, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site situated in the central part of Sri Lanka. This rugged terrain is rich in biodiversity and is considered a hotspot for various wildlife species, including leopards. The dense forests and rocky outcrops create suitable conditions for leopards to thrive. The elusive nature of leopards makes sightings a thrilling experience for those exploring the trails and viewpoints in the Knuckles.

Sinharaja Forest Reserve

Sinharaja, a tropical rainforest in the southwestern part of Sri Lanka, is renowned for its high levels of endemism and biodiversity. While leopards are not as commonly sighted here compared to other species, the reserve is still considered one of the habitats for Sri Lankan leopards. The dense vegetation and abundance of prey species contribute to the presence of leopards in this pristine forest. Visitors to Sinharaja may need to rely on patience and keen observation to catch a glimpse of these elusive predators.

Threats to Leopards

Human-Wildlife Conflict

Human-wildlife conflict poses a significant threat to leopards in Sri Lanka. As their habitats overlap with human settlements, conflicts arise, leading to negative consequences for both leopards and communities. Conservation efforts include implementing community-based initiatives and secure livestock practices.

Illegal Wildlife Trade

The illegal wildlife trade is a grave threat to leopards, driven by demand for their skins and body parts. This illicit trade contributes to population decline. Conservation measures involve strict law enforcement, awareness campaigns, and collaboration with international organizations.
